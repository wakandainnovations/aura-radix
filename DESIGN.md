## Overview

Aura is a **dark, command-style analytics workspace** built for public-relations and media teams who monitor mentions, sentiment, and crisis signals at a glance. The product personality is **calm, data-forward, and operational**: surfaces are restrained so charts, feed items, and threat callouts can carry the emotional weight. A cool blue undercurrent in panels and focus states pairs with a near-black backdrop and off-white type for a modern console aesthetic—serious, legible, and comfortable in long sessions. **Translucent** surface treatment on floating panels and menus lets the dark canvas read through slightly, so depth comes from **layering and blur** as much as from color steps.

## Colors

**Neutrals and structure.** The canvas, cards, and popover shells share a **deep blue-black** background. Primary text is **near white**; secondary and meta text use a **cool mid gray** for a clear but quiet hierarchy. **Hairline boundaries** in blue-slate, not white, so dense dashboards never feel like a field of white rules.

**Inverted “primary” fill.** The strongest filled actions (navigation selection, key button) use **inverted** contrast: a **light** fill with **dark** type. That keeps CTAs scannable in peripheral vision without relying on a saturated brand hue for every button.

**Risk and attention.** A four-step **severity ramp** (critical red through high orange, medium amber, low green-yellow) is used for operational metrics and badging. It must stay consistent: same meaning everywhere for crisis counts, risk labels, and bot-signal callouts.

**Opinion and tone.** Sentiment uses **green** (supportive), **red** (hostile), **violet** (sarcastic or non-literal), and a **slate** neutral so neutral never reads as “missing data.”

**Tints, not full backgrounds.** Sub-metrics, warning strips, and optional chips use **tinted fills** (roughly 10% color at 2px borders) to preserve the dark field while still encoding category.

**Charts.** Axes and grids stay **low-contrast gray** so data ink dominates. **Multi-entity** comparison lines rotate through distinct, saturated colors per entity; legend rows mirror the same swatches. Tooltips use a **very dark** body with a **1px** neutral border and white primary labels for readability on top of the chart.

**Positive / success moments.** A **green** treatment for success toasts; **caution** yellow for “cluster” or cross-entity mode badges, always as badge chrome—not full surfaces.

**Hero verdict moments.** An occasional **emerald gradient** panel can signal a strong “market verdict” or strategic fit; keep these rare so they read as a headline insight, not default chrome.

## Typography

**Stack.** The product uses a **system sans-serif** stack: native UI fonts and hinting, tuned for data tables and long comment threads. There is no custom display typeface: clarity and loading speed outrank character.

**Hierarchy.** The **page title** is short and bold. **Card titles and chart titles** are **semibold, small-to-medium**—large enough to scan, small enough to fit dense grids. **Metric values** jump with **24px–36px** bold weight for the numbers that matter in crisis. **All-caps, tracked labels** mark secondary dimensions (KPIs, time grain, “impact” groupings) without competing with the main value.

**Legibility features.** **OpenType** contextual alternates and ligature flags stay on for clean punctuation in long-form and quoted social copy.

**Chart text.** **12px** ticks; labels stay in **muted** gray. Chart titles match **card heading** size so a screen of charts does not look shouty.

## Layout

**Shell.** A **fixed left rail** holds navigation and app identity; the rest is **scrolling** analysis and feed. A **header strip** on top of the main area carries global context (title, active entities, time controls). A **resizable** or fixed **inspector** can sit at the right edge of some flows, separated by a **vertical edge** the same as card borders.

**Grid.** Content uses an **8px base** rhythm: gaps of **8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 32** and card padding of **20–32** for analytics blocks. **KPI rows** often use **quartered** columns on wide screens, collapsing on narrow.

**Data density.** Tables, feeds, and comment threads favor **tight but breathable** line spacing: **4px and 8px** vertical gaps in lists, **6px and 8px** padding on compact controls.

**Sticky chrome.** The analytics **header** can be **sticky** with a z-layer above the scroll region so entity and filter context is always in view on long pages.

**Modal flows.** **Centered** dialogs; **very large** crisis or comparison surfaces may use a **nearly full-viewport** frame with **critical-strength** border emphasis.

**Layering:** regular content **(z ~ 0)**, **sticky** sub-headers **(z 10)**, **overlay** and **modals (z 50)**, **menus and typeahead (z 100+)**, and rare **highest** stacking for full-screen choosers.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth is **layered, not dramatic**. The default is **flat**: card against background, separated by **1px** border. **Interaction** uses a light shadow on hover for metric tiles, a soft large shadow for elevated menus, and the heaviest dialog shadow plus a strong border for modals. **Scrim** overlays are **~80%** dark with **4px** blur; they calm the busy dashboard behind a dialog.

**No strong inner shadows** on the default set; the **raised** look comes from **ring focus** and **slightly lifted** popovers, not bevels.

**Motion as signal.** **Pulse** and **flashing** animations are **reserved** for time-sensitive or high-severity context so they do not desensitize operators.

## Shapes

**Default radius** is a **0.5rem (8px)** **rounded** shape on most cards, fields, and buttons. **Larger, softer** corners appear on **wider** marketing-style panels. **Pills and filters** are **fully rounded** only where the control is a **chip, badge, or single-line filter**. **Square or minimal radius** is acceptable in dense tables when alignment matters.

**Crisis emphasis:** **stronger** border weight or **2px** outline on a **red** keyline for **alert-class** modals, distinct from everyday dividers.

## Components

**Navigation.** A **vertical** list: **icon + label**, **12–20px** horizontal padding. **Active** is **inverted** fill; **inactive** is **muted** with **hover** to **tinted** **accent** background. **Nested** items are **indented** with a **left rule** the same as dividers, **one step smaller** in type. **Sub-active** can use a **tinted primary** text instead of a full block fill.

**Cards and blocks.** The standard **data card** is **bordered**, same background as the shell, **1px** outline. **Elevated** cards add **softer** border and **deeper** shadow. **KPI** stats often use **tinted** **border + background** by category (green, red, or purple stat panels).

**Buttons.** **Primary** is **tall (40px)**, **rounded**, **inverted** colors, **reduced** opacity on hover. **Primary-outline** and **add** actions use a **tinted** light fill with a **luminant** **border** so they are secondary to the solid primary. **Ghost** and **icon** actions use **circular** hit areas on a **transparent** or **tinted-red** **hover** for destructive-adjacent controls.

**Selects, filters, and command entry.** Triggers are **40px** height, **16px** horizontal padding, full-width **min** from label; menus **drop** with **bordered** panel and **shadow-l**.

**Modals and dialogs.** **Centered**, **24px** body padding, **2xl** shadow, **1px** **border**, **title** at **18px** bold, **dismiss** as **circular** ghost. **Form fields** are **40px** with **2px** **focus** **ring** offset to **card** background. **Error** callouts: **pale** red field with **1px** **red-30** border, **red** text.

**Feeds and mentions.** Rows or cards use **small** **badge** for **threat** with **tinted** **backgrounds**; **right-click** / **context** menus are **token-popover** styled.

**Charts.** **Dashed** grid, **2px** stroke for **trend** lines, **no dots** at default for cleaner overlays; **legends** below in **12px** and **tinted** small cells for **per-entity** key.

**Toast / success.** **Icon-forward** (check), **bouncy** entrance allowed for **acknowledgment**—keep **short** duration and **return** to calm UI.

**Skeleton loading.** **Neutral** **pulse** on blocks matching **text** and **value** line heights so layout does not jump.

**Stat cards and tooltips.** **Metric** label in **all-caps**; **value** in **2xl** bold. **Info** **tooltips** are **small** **popover** on **hint** **icon** hover, **w-48** text width, **2px** padding in body, **default** **shadow**.

## Do's and Don'ts

- **Do** keep the **background** and **default card** the **same** tone so the scene reads as a **single** canvas. Use **borders and spacing** to separate, not a lighter “card white.”
- **Do** use **threat** and **sentiment** **tokens** consistently: **one** **meaning** **per** color family.
- **Do** use **tinted** **surfaces** and **1–2px** **borders** for **badges** and **tags**; avoid **solid** **flood** **fills** except for **strong** CTA and **active** **nav** selection.
- **Do** use **inverted** **primary** for **highest** **commit** **actions** in dark mode so legibility holds without extra **saturation**.

- **Do not** use **pulsing** or **flashing** **animation** for **static** or **low-priority** elements.
- **Do not** mix **arbitrary** **hues** for the **same** **semantic** **metric** in different **views** (e.g., **“critical”** should always map to the **red** **critical** **token**).
- **Do not** let **decorative** **emerald** **or** **yellow** **treatments** **repeat** in **every** **row**—**reserve** for **verdicts**, **wins**, or **true** **warnings** so the UI stays **credible** and **calm**.
